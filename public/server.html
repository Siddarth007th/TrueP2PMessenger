🧅 How the Adaptive Self-Healing Protocol Works

1
Smart Threat Simulation — The "Simulate Smart Wipe" button deletes records from BOTH your My Work (owned messages) AND your Locker (blind vault copies). This simulates an intelligent attacker who targets all data, not just your own messages.
2
Dual Anomaly Detection — Every 5 seconds, the anomaly detector compares the live database against two separate registries: one for My Work, one for Locker. If records vanish from either without a cryptographic signature, separate threat events fire immediately.
3
Onion-Routed Recovery — Missing IDs are wrapped in 3-layer AES-256-GCM onion encryption and routed through the peer mesh. My Work data is recovered from peers who hold blind copies. Locker data is recovered from the nodes who are actually party to those conversations.
4
Exponential Vault Replication (2→4→8) — After each threat, the replication factor DOUBLES. Recovered data is spread across ALL peer lockers via onion routing. With a 4-node mesh (A, B, C, D), data gets replicated to every peer — ensuring maximum redundancy.
5
Rehydration — Encrypted rows return through the reverse onion path, are re-inserted into the vault, and decrypted with ECDH keys. Chat updates without page refresh. Locker rows are restored as opaque ciphertext.
